Major Historical Events

Battle Of Badr (624 CE) :


The Battle of Badr was the first major battle in Islamic history. It took place on the 17th of Ramadan, 2 AH (624 CE), near Badr between the Muslims of Madinah and the Quraysh of Makkah.

The Muslim army had only about 313 soldiers, while the Quraysh army had around 1,000 soldiers. Despite being greatly outnumbered, the Muslims placed their trust in Allah and fought bravely under the leadership of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ). By the help of Allah, the Muslims achieved a historic victory.

The Battle of Badr strengthened the Muslim community and became a symbol of faith, courage, and perseverance. It remains one of the most important events in Islamic history.

Hijrah :

The migration from Makkah to Madinah marked the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

Conquest Of Makkah :

In 630 CE, Makkah was peacefully brought under Muslim control, marking a major turning point in Islamic history.


Important Personalities :

Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ)

Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) was the final Messenger of Allah. He was born in Makkah in 570 CE and spent his life spreading Islam, teaching justice, mercy, and worship of One God. Despite hardships, he united Arabia and changed world history forever.

Abu Bakr (RA)

The first Caliph of Islam and the closest companion of the Prophet (ﷺ). He supported Islam from the beginning and preserved the unity of the Muslim Ummah after the Prophet’s passing.

Umar ibn Al-Khattab (RA)

The second Caliph known for his justice and powerful leadership. Under his rule, Islam expanded rapidly and a strong administrative system was built.
